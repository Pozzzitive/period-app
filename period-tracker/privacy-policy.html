The short version: Your health data stays on your device. We don't collect it, we don't send it to any server, and we don't share it with anyone. Period.

1. Who We Are (Data Controller)

Period Tracker ("the App") is developed and published by [Your Name], an independent developer based in the European Union.

For any privacy-related questions, contact us at [your-email@example.com].

2. Lawful Basis for Processing

We process your data under the following lawful bases (GDPR Article 6 and Article 9):

  • Health data (Article 9(2)(a)): Your explicit consent, which you provide during onboarding. You choose which categories of health data to track (cycle data, symptoms/moods, intercourse logs). You can withdraw consent at any time in Settings → Data Consent.
  • Purchase data (Article 6(1)(b)): Necessary for the performance of your purchase contract with Apple or Google.
  • Advertising data (Article 6(1)(a)): Your consent via the App Tracking Transparency prompt (iOS) or advertising preferences (Android). You can withdraw this at any time.

3. Our Core Principle

Period Tracker is built on a local-first, privacy-first architecture. The App is fully functional without an internet connection and without creating an account. All health and personal data is stored exclusively on your device.

4. Data We Process

4.1 Health & Personal Data (stored locally only)

The following data is stored only on your device and is never transmitted to us or any third party:

  • Menstrual cycle dates and predictions
  • Symptoms, moods, and daily logs
  • Fertility tracking data (if enabled)
  • Medication and contraception reminders
  • Health conditions you choose to track
  • Custom symptom tags
  • Photos attached to log entries (stored in the app's local sandbox)
  • App lock PIN (stored as a salted SHA-256 hash in your device's secure Keychain/Keystore)

Important We have zero access to this data. We cannot read, recover, or reset it. If you uninstall the App, this data is permanently deleted.

4.2 Purchase Information

If you make an in-app purchase (one-time app purchase or Premium Plus subscription), the transaction is handled entirely by Apple (App Store) or Google (Google Play). We receive only a purchase confirmation token to unlock features. We do not receive your payment details, billing address, or financial information.

4.3 Advertising

The free version of the App may show ads provided by Google AdMob. AdMob may collect:

  • Device advertising identifier (IDFA on iOS, GAID on Android)
  • General device information (model, OS version)
  • IP address (for approximate location to serve relevant ads)

AdMob never has access to your health data. If you purchase the App (one-time purchase), all ads are permanently removed. For users in Teenager Mode (under 18), ads are served with child-directed treatment enabled: only non-personalized, age-appropriate (G-rated) ads are shown, and no tracking or behavioral profiling occurs.

On iOS, the App requests your permission via the App Tracking Transparency (ATT) prompt before loading any ad. If you decline, only non-personalized ads are shown.

You can opt out of personalized advertising:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy → Tracking → disable for Period Tracker
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalization

See Google's privacy policy: policies.google.com/privacy

5. Data We Do NOT Collect

We want to be explicit about what we never do:

  • We do not collect, transmit, or store your health data on any server
  • We do not use analytics services that track your health information
  • We do not sell, rent, or share any personal data with third parties
  • We do not create user profiles or behavioral models
  • We do not use Facebook SDK, social media trackers, or similar tracking tools
  • We do not infer pregnancy or medical conditions from your data

7. Consent Withdrawal

Under GDPR Article 7(3), you can withdraw your consent at any time. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

  • Health data consent: Go to Settings → Data Consent to disable specific data categories (symptoms, intercourse). Disabling a category hides the logging UI. To delete existing data, use Settings → Data Management.
  • Advertising consent: Revoke tracking permission in your device's privacy settings (see Section 4.3).

8. Children's Privacy

The App includes a Teenager Mode for users aged 9-17, which hides age-inappropriate content (intercourse logging, fertility tracking). In compliance with GDPR Article 8, users under 16 should have parental consent to use the App.

Users in Teenager Mode receive only non-personalized, G-rated ads with child-directed treatment enabled. No tracking or behavioral profiling occurs for these users. The App does not knowingly collect personal data from children — all data remains on-device regardless of user age.

9. Your Rights (GDPR)

Since all data is stored locally on your device, you have full and direct control:

  • Access (Article 15): All your data is visible within the App at any time
  • Export / Portability (Article 20): Export your data as JSON or PDF from Settings → Data Management
  • Rectification (Article 16): Edit any logged entry directly in the App
  • Erasure (Article 17): Delete all data from Settings → Data Management → Delete All Data, or by uninstalling the App
  • Restriction (Article 18): Disable specific data categories in Settings → Data Consent
  • Withdraw consent (Article 7(3)): Withdraw consent at any time in Settings → Data Consent

Because we do not collect or store your data on any server, there is nothing for us to delete on our end. Your device is the sole location of your data.

10. Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe your data protection rights have been violated, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. You can contact the supervisory authority in the EU member state of your habitual residence, place of work, or place of the alleged infringement.

A list of EU data protection authorities is available at: edpb.europa.eu
